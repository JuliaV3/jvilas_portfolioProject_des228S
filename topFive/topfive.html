Top 5's

- Places That I Want Design To Take ME:


Tokyo

I've always liked Japan. When I visited a year ago, I was completely taken in by the food, the collectibles, the lights, and the energy.

Tokyo felt like a more organized version of New York: chaotic yet beautiful, with towering buildings, neon signs, and bold fashion choices.

Beyond aesthetics, Japan has a deep cultural appreciation for design, especially in visual communication and product innovation.

It’s definitely a place where I’d love to try my luck and grow as a designer.


Sydney

I remember watching a segment years ago about how Sydney is known for its advancements in technology and innovation.

To be honest, I was more fascinated by the idea of everyday encounters with wild animals.

Still, from a professional standpoint, Sydney has a strong focus on visual design and UX, making it a city I’d seriously consider for design work.


Seattle

Seattle stands out for its balance of creativity and tech. With companies like Microsoft, Amazon, and countless startups based there,

i's a great environment for designers interested in digital innovation.

The city’s appreciation for clean, functional design also makes it an inspiring place to grow professionally.


Singapore

Singapore is one of Southeast Asia’s leading tech and design capitals.

It blends precision, innovation, and cultural diversity, making it a fascinating place to work in UX/UI, branding, or motion design.

It’s also a visually stunning city — and to be honest, I can’t help but think of “Crazy Rich Asians” every time it comes to mind.


New York

New York has always felt like the heart of creativity and complete chaos.

show “Sex and the City” captures the fast pace of daily life, and the city is bursting with artistic energy

from fashion shows and gallery openings to immersive club events.

Opportunities span across advertising, digital media, animation, and more.

It’s the kind of place that challenges and inspires designers to do their best work, and you need to be your best.

I’d love to experience New York as a designer, but only once I feel truly confident in my skills.
