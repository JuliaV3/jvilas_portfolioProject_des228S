Andy Warhol

Visit the Warhol Foundation

Andy Warhol is a designer I admire because of how bold and ahead of his time he was.

His work didn't just make a statement—it changed the way people saw both art and design.

As a pioneer of the Pop Art movement, he turned everyday things like soup cans and famous faces into colorful,

eye-catching pieces that made people think.

I love how he used bright colors, repetition, and imagery from mass media to create something entirely new.

Warhol had a way of mixing commercial and fine art that still feels fresh today,

and his influence is everywhere from ads to album covers to modern branding.


Jackson Wang

Jackson Wang

Jackson Wang is an absolutely amazing and talented singer. I used to listen to

him back when he was still with the K-pop group GOT7, and I’ve admired him ever

since. He’s someone who has worked incredibly hard for what he wanted.

Originally from China, he chose to pursue music in Korea, even though he didn’t

know much Korean at the time. Through dedication and effort, he not only became

fluent in Korean but also speaks Mandarin, Cantonese, and English.

Now with a successful solo career, Jackson is loved not just for his music but for

his beautiful, respectful, and goofy personality. He also has his own brand and

plays an active role in directing his music videos, which I really admire. I simply

love the way he expresses himself through his art and music.


Naoko Takeuchi

Naoko Takeuchi

Naoko Takeuchi is the Japanese manga artist best known for creating Sailor Moon, one of the most iconic magical girl series of all time.

I first watched Sailor Moon when I was around 10 or 11 years old and have loved it ever since. The anime originally aired in the early 1990s,

and it quickly became a favorite among fans worldwide.

Naoko Takeuchi is a designer I admire because of how she created a world that was both magical and relatable.

Her characters were strong, stylish, and had real emotions, which made them feel like friends.

She blended fantasy with everyday life in a way that was both fun and meaningful.

Takeuchi's work has inspired countless artists and designers, showing that you can mix beauty with powerful storytelling.

Her influence can be seen in everything from fashion to animation, making her a true icon in the design world.

For me, Sailor Moon was fun and it was one of the most memorable romances I watched growing up.
